Projects

  • "Twitter Feeds and Surge Modeling", 2016 Supervisor : Prof. Emmanuel Gobet, Department of Applied Mathematics, Ecole Polytechnique. With Laurent Meunier, I developed a probabilistic model for social networks dynamics. Detecting significant changes in networks flows, such as temporary surges in traffic, is of significant practical importance for maintaining server reliability, and predicting "trendy" topics in advance. The objective of this work was to model the dynamics of "viral" tweets and the peer-to-peer propagation of ideas and moods, and compare it to an exegeneous flow of messages. By combining established results on branching processes and recent work by Simma & Jordan, we build a model based on point processes and simulate its evolution using Python. The specific point processes used, named Hawkes Processes, were introduced in 1971 to model earthquakes that happened in cascades. We also develop a model to account for specific features that are linked with the more popular tweets. Finally, using rare-event simulation techniques such as importance sampling, we estimate the probability of a "Twitpocalypse", namely, that 1 billion messages are emitted within 24 hours.   • "Bitcoin Trading", 2015

    Nowadays, a major part of stock market trading is entirely electronic and relies on quantitative strategies.

    Many of these strategies use current and past historical data about the market to obtain a price prediction. Depending on the predicted value for the stock price, a buy/sell/wait action is taken. However, there are few strategies which quantitatively make use of qualitative data, such as news reports and relevant social network data.

    This work involved statistical analysis of historical stock prices to implement an algorithmic trading strategy for the Bitcoin, an electronic crypto-currency.

    The main algorithm is adapted from recent work by Devavrat Shah and Kang Zhang. The results were validated with extensive backtests.

    Next, I extended the algorithm with an additional predictor that relies on Twitter sentiment analysis. I set up a framework that retrieves and analyzes relevant Twitter messages, and averages the sentiment with time sliding windows to obtain a sentiment indicator.

    By putting together the previous statistical algorithm and the Twitter sentiment, we obtain a new price predictor.

    The results were again validated with backtests, confirming the advantage of using both statistical and qualitative data.

  • "Protein Structure Classification", 2016

    Supervisor : Prof. Frank Nielsen, Department of Computer Science, Ecole Polytechnique

    Recently, bioscientists have developed new protein structures through computer simulations.

    Within the CASP Project,one important area of research is to find which of these simulated structures, if any, can be found in natural environments.

    We use hierarchical clustering and classification techniques to assess large numbers of protein structures.

    Furhermore, we develop parallel versions of the C++ algorithms using the MPI interface.

  • "Group Theory : Applications of Polya's Counting Theorem", 2015

    Supervisor : David Hernandez, Department of Mathematics, Ecole Polytechnique

    Consider the following combinatorics problem : how many colorings of a cube are there with 3 colors, up to a rotation ?

    This question, and many other counting problems that arise from everyday life, can be solved using Polya's powerful counting theorem.

    This short work revisits Polya's theorem and applies it to solve various counting problems from geometry and ring theory.
