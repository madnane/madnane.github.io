Projects

  • "Adiabatic Quantum Control of the Schroedinger Equation", 2016
    Supervisor : Prof. Ugo Boscain, Department of Applied Mathematics, Ecole Polytechnique.
    Research in quantum control has shown its impact in various fields such as Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (NMR), photochemistry or even in the design of quantum gates in quantum computing. I was involved in this research while working with Prof. Ugo Boscain at Polytechnique’s Center of Applied Mathematics (CMAP) in 2016. I was mainly concerned with the problem of controlling the Schrödinger equation via conical intersections of the eigenvalues of the Hamiltonian. In this project, I focused on the study of a two-level system. I developed a constructive method to control the bilinear Schrödinger equation via two controls, in specific cases, when the system is closed (without a coupling with the environment). In that case I provided accurate analytical solutions to the equation. I found out that one approach could consist in designing a slow control path that passes through eigenvalues intersections in the space of controls allowing exchanges of probability between the intersecting levels, a strategy that takes advantage of the adiabatic theory. In case when the quantum system is coupled with the environment, I had to numerically simulate the behavior of the norm of the wave function and evaluates the dissipation amount for different control paths. Which enabled me to assess the relevancy and effectiveness of each control.

  • "Bitcoin Trading", 2015
    Nowadays, a major part of stock market trading is entirely electronic and relies on quantitative strategies. Many of these strategies use current and past historical data about the market to obtain a price prediction. Depending on the predicted value for the stock price, a buy/sell/wait action is taken. However, there are few strategies which quantitatively make use of qualitative data, such as news reports and relevant social network data.
    This work involved statistical analysis of historical stock prices to implement an algorithmic trading strategy for the Bitcoin, an electronic crypto-currency.
    The main algorithm is adapted from recent work by Devavrat Shah and Kang Zhang. The results were validated with extensive backtests.
    Next, I extended the algorithm with an additional predictor that relies on Twitter sentiment analysis. I set up a framework that retrieved and analyzed relevant Twitter messages, and averaged the sentiment with time sliding windows to obtain a sentiment indicator. By putting together the previous statistical algorithm and the Twitter sentiment, we obtained a new price predictor. The results were again validated with backtests, confirming the advantage of using both statistical and qualitative data.

  • "Protein Structure Classification", 2016
    Supervisor : Prof. Frank Nielsen, Department of Computer Science, Ecole Polytechnique.
    Recently, bioscientists have developed new protein structures through computer simulations. Within the CASP Project,one important area of research is to find which of these simulated structures, if any, can be found in natural environments. We used hierarchical clustering and classification techniques to assess large numbers of protein structures.
    Finally, I implemented parallel versions of the C++ algorithms using the MPI interface.

  • "Group Theory : Applications of Polya's Counting Theorem", 2015
    Supervisor : David Hernandez, Department of Mathematics, Ecole Polytechnique.
    Consider the following combinatorics problem : how many essentially different ways are there of colouring the faces of a cube using 3 colors ?
    This question, and many other counting problems that arise from everyday life, can be solved using Polya's powerful counting theorem. This short work revisits Polya's theorem and applies it to solve various counting problems from geometry and ring theory.
